## Basics of the DLC

I'm assuming you've probably done *some* of the DLC, or seen marketing materials or trailers. However, here's a quick summary of the basics.

### Portals

The DLC primarily revolves around entering portals to Hyperspace Lumiose, an alternative dimension with similar layout to portions of Lumiose City but with multiple differences:

- The Pokémon that can spawn here include things that can't be found in Lumiose.
- All Pokémon have levels above 100.
- You can find new berries, Gimmighoul coins, and sometimes rare items or rare balls.
- You get a set of missions when you enter a zone.
	- Completing missions gives Hyperspace Survey points, which are initially needed for story progression, but later allow you to access special scans for 5-star portals.

Portals are shown on your map - and you can press the minus button to show only the portals. You can't fast travel to the portals directly, but there are enough fast travel spots in Lumiose that this isn't a problem. Portals **can** be inside Wild Zones, inside the nightly Battle Zones, and on roofs.

There are three types of portal you'll see:

- Wild Zones with green icons: similar to the Wild Zones of the city, Pokémon spawn here for you to catch
	- Catching Pokémon from these zones will revert them to a normal level, rather than them being level 120 or whatever.
- Battle Zones with red icons: similar to Battle Zones of the city, trainers are here for battling
	- Note that Hyperspace trainers do not give money (directly or via prize medals), but they can occasionally give berries, bottle caps, and rare balls
- Special zones with yellow question marks: these usually contain side missions, but sometimes special Pokémon
- Mega battles with the mega evolution icon: you'll fight a mega here, either for story missions, side battles for new mega stones, or a rogue mega rematch

Portals come with a number of stars (which I also call 'tiers'), from 1 to 5, indicating the difficulty of the portal. Higher tier portals have higher levelled Pokémon and rewards, and you get less time in them - Wild Zones generally have rarer Pokémon, and Battle Zones have stronger trainers (which start mega evolving their Pokémon too). Portals (other than special and mega) show the size of Zone, and what type of Pokémon are inside, so you can prepare your team before entering. Wild Zone portals also show a preview of up to 3 species you'll find in there, though medium and large Zones can have more than 3 species present; higher tier portals can also have evolved versions spawing.

Within Hyperspace, you don't find the usual Pokéball-shaped items on the ground; instead, small and large Pokéballs float around in various places of the Hyperspace Zone. You can use any damaging move to smash these and collect items from within - primarily Exp. Candies and Hyperspace berries. Completing all of the missions in an Zone causes a large golden Bonus Ball to appear somewhere, which has extra rewards when smashed. Battle Zones usually spawn less balls, though they are still present, and special Zones don't spawn balls. Throughout Hyperspace there is also **a lot** of mega shard patches to smash.

The map in Hyperspace doesn't show the layout of the area you're in, but you can still fast travel to the entrance portal from it (only when not in battle). You can access your boxes and satchel as normal in Hyperspace (boxes only when not in battle, satchel invokes a cooldown if you are in battle). You can't enter Link Play in Hyperspace. You can't use Exp. Candies, Rare Candies, or vitamins in Hyperspace, nor evolve anything.

### Donuts

To enter portals, you have to feed Ansha's Hoopa friend a donut - which Ansha will make for you at Hotel Z from your berries. To begin with you only have the regular berries (findable and purchasable throughout the city) and can use up to 3 in a donut. As you progress, each new rogue mega you take down allows you to use another berry, reaching the maximum of 8 eventually. Within Hyperspace, instead of finding regular berries you find Hyper berries, which are much more powerful. Once you have enough Hyper berries, you essentially won't be using regular berries anymore.

Berries and donuts have a few associated numbers:

- Level boost: increases your Pokémon's levels, which can go up to level 200. This will allow you to compete with the Pokémon in Hyperspace
- Calories: allows you to stay in Hyperspace for longer
	- You will find that higher tier portals drain calories much, much faster than lower tier ones, so you really do need to be using 4- or 5-star donuts for 4- and 5-star portals. You will find that a 5-star donut gives you about 6 minutes in a 5-star portal and about 8-9 minutes in a 4-star one, but pushing 90 minutes in a 1-star portal; conversely a 1-star donut gives under 30 seconds in a 5-star portal and under a minute in a 4-star portal.
- Flavour score: berries contribue to one or more of the 5 flavours (Sweet, Spicy, Sour, Bitter, Fresh), which determines what pool of buffs can be pulled from, and how strong they are. The resulting donut has a total flavour score to give an indication of how strong it is (it is just a sum of the input flavours).
- Stars: contributing powerful enough berries will upgrade the donut's number of stars. The above three values get multiplied by 10% per star (so a 5-star donut gets a 1.5x multiplier).
	- Using a donut that is below the tier of the portal gives a catch chance penalty (0.9x if it is 1 star lower, and 0.3x if it is 2 or more stars lower - this is actually way bigger nerf than it appears due to how the rest of the calculation works).

There are details later on the page about what each flavour and buff does etc.

### Surviving Hyperspace

You leave Hyperspace in 4 ways:

- You can manually leave via the entrance portal at any time
- You will be forced out when you run out of calories
	- Opening any menu (X-menu, the map, or entering dialogue) pauses the timer
- You will be forced out if your party is all fainted simultaneously
	- You can of course uses revives (if your satchel action isn't on cooldown), or swap fainted Pokémon for unfainted ones in your boxes (when outside of battle)
- You will be forced out if you take enough damage to black out

Notably, when you leave Hyperspace, anything in-progress is instantly cancelled. This includes:

- If you were battling a trainer, the battle is cancelled - it is not won unless you saw the VICTORY text
- If you were catching a Pokémon, it won't be caught until the animation finishes with the ball 'clicking' - if the animation is at 2 shakes when you left, that is not caught
	- Spawned shinies are despawned
- If you were smashing a floating ball, it won't be considered smashed unless the move collided with it and the ball exploding animation played
	- If you had just smashed a ball and were in the process of getting items when leaving, you did get all the items from it - the notifications continue to play when you're back in Lumiose
- Unfinished missions are thrown out
	- If you finished all missions but failed to find the Bonus Ball, unlucky, its gone

As for general tips on surviving Hyperspace, I suggest using any stock of Exp./Rare Candies you have to bring your team to level 100 (you get a lot from Hyperspace anyway), and also EV train them (you can reset their EVs for 5 mega shards in the lobby of the Rust Syndicate, then buy vitamins at any PokéCenter - or trade mega shards for vitamin feathers from a guy in an alley near WZ-17). Give your team good moves, and don't be afraid to mix up movesets for other types. I have like 15 EV-trained Pokémon to provide coverage over all sorts of types; I usually went 252 in the relevant offense, and either 252 HP (alphas come with 252 HP EVs already) or spread around defences and speed to accomodate for weaknesses (use Bulbapedia to check their base stats), and then applied an Adamant (+Att -SpAtt) or Modest (+SpAtt -Att) mint (you can purchase both in the little square between WZ-3 and WZ-4, near Sushi High Roller).

You will also want environmental moves, so you need access to things that can smash boulders (strong 'crushing' moves), clear brambles ('cutting' or 'burning' moves), and clear sludge (water moves or strong special psychic moves). I would also suggest having a few attacks that hit their target directly at range, rather than needing to move to it or fire a projectile - the main moves I use are Confusion, Psychic, Thunderbolt, Heat Wave, Blizzard, and Hurricane (though there are more). This allows you to easily hit floating Pokéballs at range and over obstacles.

Hyperspace is also rife with smashable mega shards. If you haven't bought out Quarsitico, this will really accelerate it; once you have, there is a man in an alley near WZ-17 that trades new TMs and vitamin feathers for mega shards. You will probably max out your mega shards very quickly if you have the Canari plushes (2 or 3 portals are enough if you try), so you'll probably visit him often. Wide-area moves are good for big patches of mega shards - things like Earthquake, Bulldoze, Ancient Power, Dazzling Gleam, Blizzard, Heat Wave, Thousand Waves, etc.

# Shinies in PLZA
First, some basics on shinies

- The sound and animation are back! After being absent in S/V, the bliblibling-sound and sparkle animation are back.
	- For some details: the sound and animation play periodically while you are near enough for the Pokémon to be spawned (and hear their cries), even if you can't currently see the Pokémon (e.g. its on a roof, through a wall, etc). When you first get close to the (like within a 1-roll distance), the sound and animation will play once to make it obvious.
	- While close, the shiny will also have passive small sparkles surrounding them. It may be hard to spot unless you're actively looking, but it does make some less obvious shinies clearer.
- Shinies **can** faint and will disappear if you fail to catch them in the catch window. I am not sure if shinies can run away & disappear, if it one of the spook-able species (eg Fennekin).
- Shinies have a 3x multiplier in their catch rate calculation. Due to how this is used, the overall actual catch chance massively increased (roughly 8x) - though still not guaranteed.
	- Example, full HP unaware Alakazam in Lumiose with no plushes is 3.06% with a regular ball, and an alpha is 0.83%; shiny versions are 24.17% and 6.63% respectively.
	- Even in the catch window they are not guaranteed! Make sure to save before KOing one! (The combined multipliers from 0 HP + catch window + shiny makes the catch chance very high, but it isn't always guaranteed - especially for stronger Pokémon with weaker balls, and especially for Beast Balls. Beast Balls' nerf is so large is still takes a lot of tries to catch stuff with it.)
- Base shiny chance is the same as modern normal games, i.e. 1/4096.
- You get the shiny charm by reaching research level 50 (completing the pokédex is not enough). Like other games it gives 3 additional rolls, which brings the base chance down to 1/1024.38.
	- If you don't have the DLC, there are ~1900 research points spare by completing all the base game tasks. However, the final 'complete trainer battles' task (1000 battles) is worth 2000 points - making it mandatory to get the charm. Depending on how much you farm battle zones through the story, you may have like 10 hours of battle grinding to get to that - I was at like 350 battles by the end of the story. This sucks, and to be honest don't bother.
	- If you do have the DLC, after like an hour (or less) you unlock additional hyperspace-specific research tasks, so you can get the remaining points quickly. There aren't any new research rewards past level 50 so you don't have to worry about the battles task.
- When a shiny spawns in Lumiose, it'll always be spawned. You can save and reload, travel elsewhere, whatever, it'll be there when you come back (time of day may matter, e.g. Scyther only spawn in WZ-13 at night). The game will remember the last 10 shinies, and if more than that are spawned it'll remove the oldest to make room. I don't know how this interacts with Hyperspace.
- The following Pokémon are shiny locked:
	- All rogue mega Pokémon
	- All mythical Pokémon
	- All legendary Pokémon except the few mentioned below
	- All Pokémon as a direct part of a mission or involving a special scene transition
	- In-game NPC trades and gifts, excluding fossils
	- WZ-1 Mareep, until you complete the side mission 'A Shiny Mareep'
- Notable Pokémon that **can** be shiny:
	- Revived fossils - but the shiny charm does not work on them (so 1/4096). They can also be alpha (1% chance).
	- All Pokémon in Wild Zones and throughout the city, including the always-alpha spawns and the random alphas spawn in the middle of WZ-20.
	- Legendaries in special scans
- Sparkling power donuts increase shiny chance for the specified type (see below for more details on donuts)
	- Sparkling Power donuts give additional rolls like the shiny charm - 1, 2, or 3 based on the level of the power.
		- Without shiny charm the chances are 1/2048.25, 1/1365.67, 1/1024.38
		- With shiny charm the chances are 1/819.60, 1/683.08, 1/585.57
- There is a small chance of getting a 'Catch Shiny Pokémon' mission when entering a Hyperspace Wild Zone. If you do, it will force one of the Pokémon from one of the spawners close to the portal (usually the closest spawner) to be shiny.
	- If you enter with a Sparkling Power 3 donut, this mission is guaranteed, and the forced shiny will be of the type that the donut specifies. With this, you can reroll the shiny to another from the same spawner by reloading the regular save (as long as you don't save after entering), and reroll the spawner (to likely a different species) by loading backup save and re-entering. This is definitely something you can (ab)use if you want a specific shiny.
	- Without Sparkling Power 3, you can't reroll the spawner (since you likely won't get the mission again when re-entering), but you may be able to reroll the specific Pokémon (but I didn't check that).

## Alphas and ShAlphas

Alphas return from Legends: Arceus and honestly feel more common.

- Throughout Lumiose there are a number of guaranteed alphas, that will always spawn in alpha - e.g. the Salamence in WZ-18.
- In a Wild Zone, anything that isn't guaranteed alpha has a 5% chance of becoming alpha.
- In Hyperspace, the base alpha chance is 1%, but using Alpha power donuts will increase it significantly (to 11%, 21%, and 51% for Lv1,2,3 respectively).
- Outside of Wild Zones, the 5% chance is the same, but some Pokémon can never be alpha - for example, all the Eevee outside of WZ-19 (found on roofs and stuff) can never be alpha.
- Pokémon can be both shiny and alpha, effectively meaning the base ShAlpha chance is 1/81920 for for non-guaranteed alphas, or 1/20487.6 with shiny charm - or if you get the glorious Sparkling Lv3 + Alpha Lv3 donut, a mere 1/1148.2 with shiny charm.
- Rerolling a Pokémon (see below) rerolls its alpha status - it may become alpha, or may lose alpha, when rerolled.
	- If a Pokémon is shiny, it won't be rerolled so you can't make it alpha (or not alpha).
- Alphas have 2 catch chance alterations (see [Bulbapedia](https://bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Catch_rate#Pok%C3%A9mon_Legends:_Z-A) for details, and [RotomLabs](https://rotomlabs.net/legends-z-a/catch-calculator) for a calculator):
	- It has a 0.5x multiplier just for being alpha (all else equal, this ends up being about a 60-70% reduction in catch chance - e.g. at level 50 in a regular ball, Abra is an 39% catch chance non-alpha, 11% alpha).
	- If it is fainted, it gets a 3x multiplier instead of 1.2x (effectively 1.5x since it still has the 0.5x for being alpha) (all else equal, this ends up being about 40-60% increase in catch chance - eg at level 50 in a regular ball, Abomasnow is 22% non-alpha, 33% alpha)
- Pokémon caught in missions (where there is a scene transition into the fight) are never alpha or always alpha - they don't roll for it, it is determined by the mission.
- Sizes also return, and are the usual 0-255 range. Presumably exactly 0 or 255 will be eligible for the relevant Mark in mainline games, but they can't go back to Scarlet/Violet so we can't verify yet. Teensy/Humungo power donuts limit the maximum/minimum size (see donut section for details). Alphas are presumably set to 255 size.
- All legendary and mythical Pokémon are never alpha, and always set to exactly 50% size. Every other species can be alpha.

# Saving

- You can manually save at any time and reload from right there.
- These actions create autosaves:
	- Fast travelling
	- Catching a Pokémon
	- (winning a trainer battle?)
	- Making a donut
	- Entering Hyperspace
	- (Exiting Hyperspace?)
- These actions create **backup saves**:
	- Fast travelling in Lumiose
	- Manual saves in Lumiose
- Any form of saving in Hyperspace **does not** create backup saves. At any point before arriving back in Lumiose you can reload from backup and it will put you back to before you entered Hyperspace (i.e. you haven't used the donut).
- From the main menu (you have to close the game from the switch's home menu to get there) you can press `DPAD up`, `X` and `B` at once to load a backup save. This will put you at the most recent Pokémon center you used with the game state as it was with the last backup save. This is as good as we have with PLZA to save-resetting, for some actions.
- Loading a regular save (just pressing `A` at the main menu) puts you exactly where the previous save left off. This includes if you save inside Hyperspace.

# Shiny hunting in Lumiose

- Whenever you move far enough away from a Pokémon it'll fade away. You can then run closer again and it will respawn & reroll it. This is most obvious with the alphas in WZ-20's center - running away then coming back will change what the alphas are.
	- The respawned Pokémon come from the same spawner; if you knocked out/caught one from that spawn location, it won't respawn by running away and you'll have to wait for the spawner to spawn a new one.
- Fast-travelling to anywhere - even to the location you're already at - *even if you don't move from fast-travelling to the same location* - will reroll all Pokémon. This makes hunting very fast if what you're looking for is within the spawn radius of a fast-travel point.
	- This also will not respawn Pokémon from spawners you knocked out/caught.
- The day/night transition rerolls all Pokémon. This isn't particularly fast, since you need to watch the cutscene each time, but can be useful if what you're looking for is near a bench. You can also setup something like SirToastyToes did when he hunted a shalpha scyther <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8oTZAK15QpM>
	- This also will not respawn Pokémon from spawners you knocked out/caught.
- Finally, knocking out a Pokémon will have it reroll when it is respawned, which takes 3 minutes - honestly, avoid this method.

# Hyperspace

- You autosave upon entering Hyperspace but this does not create a new backup save. Specifically, the save is basically between the spawner locations being set and the spawners rolling the specific Pokémon. This means if you reload from the autosave, the Pokémon nearby will be rerolled from the spawners (but the species of the spawners won't change).
- At any point (as long as you aren't in combat) you can fast travel back to the entrance portal. This creates a save (but not a backup save) and will reroll all Pokémon. This is an extremely good way to roll for shinies.
- If you load a backup save and re-enter the portal (with the same or a different donut):
	-  the Zone remains the same
	-  the portal remains in the same place in that Zone
	-  the possible Pokémon spawned remain the same
	-  the places Pokémon spawn **can change**
	-  your missions will change
	-  not sure if the smashable Pokéballs change position or contents

There's a handful of maps in Hyperspace, which you'll eventually learn if you do them a lot.

If you are looking for specific Pokémon in portals, it is faster to repeatedly reroll them by resting in your room in Hotel Z. Resting there as opposed to benches in the city **does not**  trigger the day-night transition cutscenes (just a shorter popup for night->day). A side benefit is that the day timer is paused while in buildings, giving you maximum time in the actual city. Remember that you can fast travel from within buildings. (Obviously if you are just resting once for the next day, you probably don't want to go into your room since that's 2 extra loading screens.)

### Large

- [Large-1] A large area surrounding the amphitheatre-like space between WZ-20 and WZ-7 (both side of the canal)
	- Teleporters go across the canal and can get you over obstructions at either end.
	- The large scaffold course at the tip of the canal (near what would be WZ-20) can be cheesed with roto-glide, you don't have to dop the intended circling.
	- There's often a teleporter or two to get on to the roofs, but there is a guaranteed ladder on the opposite side to the amphitheatre, in the clearing (accessible via tunnels)
	- Pokémon spawn areas: there's a lot
	- Side missions: multibattle with Emma vs Grisham & Griselle (#155)
- [Large-2] The block surrounding the Justice Dojo, from WZ-7 and including the canal (which is frozen over and walkable in Hyperspace)

# Donuts

## Basic info

Donuts are how you enter Hyperspace, and determine what buffs (if any) you get when you enter. Buffs are divided by flavour:

- Spicy (red) gives offensive buffs, like increased Attack, Special Attack, Speed, or damage done by specific types. This may seem less useful, but don't sleep on this bonus - it's quite noticable and very good for battle zones or rogue mega rematches.
- Bitter (blue) gives defensive buffs, like increased Defence, Special Defence, or type resistance. Like Spicy, this is great for battle zones or rogue mega rematches.
- Fresh (green) gives Encounter and Capture power buffs. These are probably the least useful for shiny hunting, since shinies have increased catch chance; and Encounter power doesn't increase the *number* of spawns, but instead reduces the time for things to respawn after being knocked-out/caught (fast travelling/running is much faster to reroll still). The capture power buff can be quite strong if you're planning on using a Beast Ball, though.
- Sour (yellow) gives Mega Power and Item buffs. Mega power buffs can increase your charge speed or reduce charge drain, which is useful but not as much as Item buffs. The Item buffs come in a few varieties: berries (big increase to the amount of berries you get), coins (for farming gimmighoul coins), treasure (nuggets & other money items), Balls (Pokéballs, including rare balls like apriballs, sport, beast, etc), and special (evolution items); you can also get Big Haul that increases the quantity of items found in the Bonus Ball. If nothing else, Berry Item Power is important for getting lots of berries so you can make better donuts, and you'll want at least 1 good Special Item Power to get the Charcadet evolution items (since you only get 1 of the 2 guaranteed from a side mission).
- Sweet (pink) is what we really want for shiny hunting, as it provides Sparkling power (shiny chance), Alpha power (alpha chance), and Humungo/Teensy power (size). 

Each power can be level 1, 2, or 3 with the magnitude of the buff increasing with each level.

- Offensive and Defensive powers are 10%, 25%, and 50% multipliers for each level
	- Resistance power is 1/0.9, 1/0.8, and 1/0.65 for each level
- Encounter power reduces respawn time (no power: 180s) to 127s, 90s, and 55s
- Catching power increases the catch chance by 10%, 20%, and 30%
- Mega power:
	- Conservation increases your mega evolution duration by 30%, 50%, and 100%
	- Charging increases the amount gained by 10%, 25%, 50%
- Size powers cap the maximum (Teensy) or minimum (Humungo) size of a Pokémon. This effectively increases the chance of a min- or max-size Pokémon (so mark-eligible) to 1/128, 1/96, and 1/32 for each level. Specifically:
	- Teensy power caps the maximum size of a spawn to 127, 95, and 31 (on a 0-255 scale)
	- Humungo power caps the minimum size of a spawn to 128, 160, and 224 (on a 0-255 scale)
- Alpha power increases the alpha chance (no power: 1%) to 11%, 21%, and 51% for each level
- Sparkling power increases shiny chance by adding 1, 2, or 3 rolls
	- Without shiny charm the chances are (no power: 1/4096), 1/2048.25, 1/1365.67, 1/1024.38
	- With shiny charm the chances are (no power: 1/1024.38), 1/819.60, 1/683.08, 1/585.57
- Big Haul power increases the Bonus Ball's rewards by +2, +3, or +5
- Item power adds weight to the chance of getting that type of item from any smashed pokéball by +1, +2, and +1000 (yes +1000, making the targetted items almost guaranteed over candy and berries)
	- Item power only applies to smashed balls and not to the sparklig items on the floor, or rewards from NPCs in battle zones
	- You also must have the relevant item power active to get anything other than berries and candies from the balls
	- The possible items depends on the tier of the Hyperspace portal entered, see table below
		- In the table, the Tier column is the tier of portal entered (number of stars) - i.e. not the tier of donut used.
		- PLUS means in addition to items from previous tiers/rows. 
		- BONUS refers to bonus balls (the big gold ones) - these do not get the additional items from item powers, except 4- and 5-star with Ball power, which adds apriballs & rare balls.
		- All tiers give berries, though the berries vary with tier (and you'll get more with Berry power, and way less with level 3 of other powers). Berries obtained in Hyperspace are always Hyper berries.
		- All tiers give Exp candies - the higher the tier, the more XP you're getting from candies on average (and you'll get more with Candy power, and way less with level 3 of other powers). Tier 1-3 drop M, L, and XL, and 4-5 only L and XL. Bonus balls only drop Rare Candy, not Exp Candy. Rogue Mega Rematch bonus balls don't drop candies at all.

## Making the best

The best recipe for making strong Shiny hunting donuts is very simple: 8 Hyper Tanga berries. This maximizes Sweet score while not having much of the others, so you'll probably always roll a Shiny power and at least one of Humungo/Teensy or Alpha power. The associated type to the Shiny power is just a straight 1/19 (18 types + All).

The best Berry hunting donut is equally as simple: 8 Hyper Kasib berries. This appears guaranteed to give you an Item power. (Big Haul power is nice but not necessary since it only applies to the Bonus Ball.) If you do get Item power, it is split equally between the 6 types (Berry, Candy, Coins, Balls, Special, Treasure).

This has 2 obvious issues: 8 of one berry is a lot, so you probably won't make many - and so you're unlikely to get the exact combination of powers you want with what you have. This has a solution: save reloading!

Making a donut creates an autosave *but not a backup save* (fast travelling to Hotel Z will probably be your backup save). Depending on how many berries you have, you may want to reload after each donut, or make a few and reroll if you don't get at least one you want. This can take a while if you're trying for Sparkling+Alpha donuts.

If you want to try for a little more time in 5-star portals, 4 Tanga + 4 Haban bumps up the calories from 3600 to 4020, moving the time from x to y. It only has 720 sweet score, and the 260 fresh score leads to some fresh buffs getting rolled - these combined do reduce the chance of Sparkling (especially 3), but its still possible to get. Similarly, 4 Kasib + 4 Roseli provide the same for sour donuts (but expect some spicy buffs). For reference, the maximum time (8 Babiri, 4800 calories) is z in a 5-star portal.

## Making rainbows

But what about all the other berries? Your best bet is to make rainbow donuts!

Rainbow donuts are donuts where two (or more) of the flavour scores **exactly** match. Rainbow donuts have some sort of buff when rolling the powers on them - experimentally they roll as if their flavour score is 30-50% higher. Of course, the downside is you're now pulling from 2 (or more) pools of buffs, so some buffs are less common. However, if you target 2 useful/synergistic buffs, you can probably make some worthwhile donuts.

For 5-star donuts, you want to aim for about 420-430 flavour score. The guide I saw focused on this, but there is benefit to using your even lower tier berries to get 2-, 3-, or 4-star donuts.

This is especially nice to make Sweet or Sour donuts with whatever berries you have (so you can save your Tanga and Kasib berries), or Spicy+Bitter donuts for battle zones and rogue mega rematches. I don't usually reload backup for these.

It is worth noting that if you do the missions and try to smash as many Pokéballs as you can, you're almost certainly going to earn more berries than you spend, even without any Item power. You can go into lower tier portals with basically whatever, but aiming for a rainbow donut can eke out some additional benefit from those lower quality berries.

Here are some example recipes (all berries are hyper berries):

- Sweet donuts for Sparkling and/or Alpha (no Tangas):
	- Note that Sparkling power is very rare under 300-400 flavour score
	- 120 Sweet+Sour (2-star): 3 Pecha, 3 Aspear, any 2 of Cheri/Chesto/Rawst
		- Cheap and cheerful. You can vary up this combination of the 5 basic berries to get different flavour combinations if you like.
	- 135 Sweet+Sour (3-star): 3 Pecha, 3 Aspear, Oran, Kelpsy/Tamato
		- 165 if you use a Qualot as the final berry
	- 225 Sweet+Fresh (4-star): 3 Chople, 2 Yache, Kebia, Shuca, Payapa
		- Has high enough bitter and sour that those creep in.
	- 430 Sweet+Fresh (5-star): Payapa, Charti, 5 Haban, any that has no (or equal) Sweet & Fresh
		- Fresh to mix in some Capture power (useful for alphas or if you are using Beast Balls or other weak balls)
		- For maximum level/time, Chilan/Babiri/Roseli give the most calories; however you can use whatever berry you have so long as it doesn't unbalance the Sweet & Fresh scores
- Spicy and/or Bitter donuts for battling:
	- 220 Spicy+Bitter (3-star): 4 Rindo, 2 Passho, 2 Rawst
	- 240 Spicy+Bitter (4-star): 4 Grepa, 4 Passho
	- 420 Spicy+Bitter (5-star): 4 Coba, 4 Charti

	- Note that Item Lv3 is very rare under 300-400 flavour score - as mentioned above, Lv3 is a lot stronger than 2

# Shiny hunting in Hyperspace

- A common way is to repeatedly fast travel to the portal, if the Pokémon you want is close enough to the portal it will spawn in when you travel. Also remember that you don't have to check every time - once a shiny is rolled, it will stay shiny, so you can travel a bunch of times in a row (very time efficient since you burn less than half a second of time per travel if you open the map quick enough), then do a quick sweep of the nearby Pokémon.
	- The main downside of this method is that every fast travel saves, so if you don't get a shiny (or as many as you wanted), you will have to load the backup save to start over - which may mean the Pokémon near the portal change, needing more backup loads.
- Running back and forth is also a good way to roll shinies, if you can find a good area to go back and forth (ideally directly toward and away from the Pokémon). You don't need to directly see the Pokémon, but it helps when you're setting up/testing for the range you're running.
	- You can also use some of the seemingly-random horizontal teleporters for this. Using a teleporter pauses the timer, so you can get even more rolls this way.
	- A benefit of this is if you setup what you want and save at the appropriate start point, you can run back and forth for a while, then check the Pokémon when your calories get to about 1/4-1/5th remaining and if it isn't shiny, load (regular) save and start again.
- At worst, you can save just outside of the Pokémon's spawn range, run into the spawn range, then quit and load (regular) save to get another roll.
	- This is the most time efficient method, since you're spending the same few seconds of calories each load to check shiny. I would only really recommend this for Switch 2 players, though, since the load times are way faster than the Switch 1.

## Sparkling power 3

Sparkling power Lv3 is special. Using one (on a wild zone) will force one of the Pokémon in one of spawners near the portal to be shiny. You will also get the `Catch Shiny Pokémon: 1` mission.

This shiny can be rerolled to another in the same spawner (i.e. potentially to/from alpha) by reloading the regular save created upon entering the portal. This allows you to try for ShAlphas, or change it from e.g. a Scyther to a Scizor to a Kleavor, if that is what you want.

In order to change the species-line that is forced, you will need to reload from backup save. When you re-enter the portal with the same donut, the species at each spawner location (may) have changed, which means the forced shiny (may) have changed (depending on how many species are available, this could take a few tries). I think the forced shiny will be at the same spawner, but I am not 100% sure on that (example: I used a Sparkling 3 donut on a Medium-1 map; the portal was always in the middle of the top of the T inside the shopping mall; the forced shiny was always at the spawner under the scaffolding-bridge, to the west of the museum).

I believe this also applies to the legendary/mythical special scan portals, but you don't get a mission so I'm not sure if it is guaranteed or I'm just lucky. The portal is far enough away from the legendaries that they cannot be forced shiny.

## Special scans

After completing the main story (mission #11) and doing a short side quest (#188), you can start doing special scans. When you earn 25,000 Hyperspace survey points, you can visit Philippe in the Rust Syndicate office to run a special scan. Running a special scan resets your Hyperspace survey points back to 0.

Only one special-scanned portal can be available at a time - if you earn 25,000 points again and get Philippe to run another scan, it will replace whatever special-scanned portal you already have. Being a 5-star portal, it Ball Power and Item Power make floating Pokéballs contain the rarest items (in addition to previous tier's items): Safari/Sport/Dream/Beast Balls, Auspicious Armour, Malicious Armour, Gold Bottle Caps. Since they're 5-star, you don't get a lot of time in there even with 5-star donuts (and very little time with under 5-star donuts).

A special scan will always be 5-star and can be one of a few things:

		- ||Eevee, Flareon, Sylveon, Leafeon, Glaceon||

	- ||Ice regionals: Frigibax/Arctibax/Baxcalibur, Hisuian Avalugg||
- A Battle Zone (of random type(s))
	- The is basically the same as the 1- to 4-star Battle Zones, but the Collector, Rich Boy, and Lady trainer types can appear which give rare balls or treasure when defeated
	- Trainers are also pretty strong and are prone to mega evolving thier Pokémon

	- Worth noting that Kecleon doesn't have any special mechanics (i.e. it is not invisible or otherwise hiding), it just spawns a pack like any other Pokémon.
	- Kecleon shows up in 2 possible 4-star normal type zones, (Kecleon Pidgeotto/Pidgeot Furfrou, Kecleon Pidgeotto/Pidgeot Kangaskhan). It is kinda annoying to get.
- Hyperspace can contain Garden pattern Vivillon, making them huntable in addition to the Meadow pattern in Lumiose. The 18 other patterns can be transferred in via HOME, but aren't catchable in-game, so back to SV for them.
- All four Squawkabilly colours can be found in normal or flying Zones (1-star+), though you may not get all 4 in a single Zone.
- Tatsugiri is in 4-star+ Zones, and can be all 3 forms; Dondozo may be present or in other Zones.
- Regional variants can appear in appropriately typed Zones:
	- 1-star+: Alolan Meowth, Galarian Meowth
	- 3-star+: Galarian Farfetch'd, Galarian Slowpoke, Galarian Mr. Mime, Galarian Yamask, Hisuian Qwilfish
	- 4-star+: Alolan Raichu, Alolan Marowak, Galarian Stunfisk
	- 5-star (special scans): Hisuian Sliggoo, Hisuian Goodra, Hisuian Avalugg
- Cleffa can appear in 2-star+ fairy Zones, making getting an alpha a lot easier than scouring roofs.
- Annoying trade, friendship, and item evolutions can appear in some Zones (that aren't elsewhere in Lumiose):
	- 3-star+: Aromatisse
	- 4-star+: Crobat, Espeon, Umbreon, Slowking, Steelix, Scizor, Slurpuff, Gourgeist, Toxtricity-Amped, Toxtricity-LowKey, Kleavor
	- 5-star (special scans): Galarian Slowbro, Galarian Slowking, Porygon2, Porygon-Z, Overqwil

Milotic, Ceruledge, and Armorouge do not appear in Hyperspace, so you will have to evolve them yourself.

Hyperspace does not count as another region, so Pikachu, Cubone, Mime Jr., and Goomy evolve into their Kantonian/Kalosian versions.

## Shiny hunting Hyperspace legendaries

Once you can start doing special scans (side mission #188), there is a chance to get special Zones containing 1 of 5 legendary Pokémon (or 2 mythicals (||meloetta|| and ||keldeo||), which cannot be shiny). The 5 legendaries (||Cobalion, Virizion, Terrakion||, ||Latias, Latios||) **can be shiny** and are huntable, ideally using using a Shiny charm and Sparkling power donuts - make sure to get an appropriate type (or all types). Below are methods for hunting them. You won't know which of these are present in the Zone until you pass through the portal, so you may need to reload a backup save to apply an appropriate donut if you guess wrong (it'll be the same legendary, i.e. if you go in with a Sparkling Power ||Dragon|| donut but you find out it is ||Virizion||, you can load the backup save to apply a ||Fighting or Grass|| donut). Other Pokémon in the Hyperspace Zone can also be shiny, and I think what spawns and where are fixed so you can farm those types if you like. Remember that catching any Pokémon creates a save, so maybe finish shiny hunting the legendary before looking at other Pokémon in the Zone.

Remember that they can break out of balls - even in the catch window - so save once they're spawned, before engaging! Their catch rate isn't as awful as regular games (or even Beldum in this game), but still low enough that they might break out.

In the provided maps, the red X is where the Pokémon spawns, the gold circle is where the portal is, the inner red area is roughly the distance you need to be to have it spawn in, the outer pink area is how far you need to move away for it to despawn, the cyan line is the route to get to it, and the dark blue line is the suggested running route for repeatedly spawning it. I have also added icons for key ladders, teleporters (with a wiggly line), bridges, and other Pokémon.

- ||Cobalion||: map Large-2, spawns on the ice near the big canal grate. Shiny colour is ||dark blue (instead of RSW-blue)||, I used a ||Beast ball (it took many attempts!)||.
	- From the portal, run straight (dropping down the ledge), down the steps, on to the frozen canal, then turn left and run along the wall all the way to the grate. Save once you see it.
	- Rerolling is a little slower than ||Virizion||, since you can't quite go directly toward and away from it. The best way is to save at the corner where you can see it, then run along the wall until the bridge, then back to the corner.
		- If you stand further away from the wall (into the center of the canal) you can more clearly see when it spawns in, so you see how far you need to go.

$img(pkmn/plza_map_large2_115.png)

- ||Terrakion||: map Large-2, spawns up on the roof near the roof fountain. Shiny colour is ||brown, red horns, blue spikes (instead of grey, brown horns, orange spikes)||, I used a ||Level|| ball.
	- From the portal run straight ahead (down the ledge) then straight to the ladder. Up the ladder, circle around until you can roto-glide over to the Justice Dojo roof, then up to the roof with the fountain then back down, and it is just over to the right from there. Save when you see it.
	- Easy respawn by heading to the nearby teleporter (which teleports you on to scaffolding near the portal), which is far enough that it despawns, then teleport back and roll forward to respawn it. You can tell it respawns because it spawns like a meter above the ground and drops down, you can just about see it when you roll in. If you happen to fall off the roof you can just reload from save.

$img(pkmn/plza_map_large2_116.png)

- ||Virizion||: map Large-2, spawns in the enclosed grassy area. Shiny colour is ||red (instead of green)||, very obvious. I used a ||Fast|| ball.
	- Getting to it is easy - from the portal run straight ahead (dropping down the ledge) then straight to the ladder. Up the ladder, then forward and right to the enclosed area.
	- Rerolling it is very easy. From the portal, turn right and go over to the wide set of stairs (there are Throh halfway down, you can KO them if you like). There is a lamppost at the top of the stairs by the edge of the area, and a lamppost by the Throh and the alley to the ladder. You want to run back and forth between these - I looped around each, but there may be a slightly shorter route. Go check when you're down to about 800 calories (or like 1/5 remaining, depending on your donut).

$img(pkmn/plza_map_large2_117.png)

- ||Latias||: map Large-1, spawns on the opposite side of the canal on the small building, the roof surrounded in barriers. Shiny colour is ||bright orange (instead of red)||, I used a ||Level|| ball.
	- Getting there:
		- From the portal go right, then enter the tunnel to the grassy clearing
		- Climb the ladder, then clamber up the roofs
		- You'll want to save when you see it, then make your way over to the roof directly to the right of the ladder as you climb it
	- Respawn by running along the roof and down to the next roof (in the direction of the portal) - you want to circle the second plant on the roof's edge, then climb back up and pass close to the second window on the inner edge, and repeat.
		- You can see it spawn in clearly.

$img(pkmn/plza_map_large1_126.png)

- ||Latios||: map Large-1, spawns atop the tallest building. Shiny colour is ||tealish green (instead of blue)||, quite obvious. I used a ||Friend|| ball.
	- There's only 1 route to it, which is:
		- From the portal go right, then enter the tunnel to the grassy clearing
		- Climb the ladder, then run across roofs to the horizontal teleporter
		- Use the teleporter to teleport across the canal
		- Make your way over the roofs and bridges to find the vertical teleporter, which takes you to the roof of the tallest building.
	- It is not spawned until you take the teleporter up, but unfortunately getting far enough away is difficult (teleporting back down is not far enough, and dropping back to the road level means you have to climb back up the long way). It is faster to save on the teleporter (before using it), teleport up, then if it is not shiny you quit the game and reload the (normal) save.

$img(pkmn/plza_map_large1_127.png)

- ||Keldeo||: map Large-3, on the raised platform surrounded by water in the enclosed area.
	- You may have to catch the legendaries from map Large-2 before this portal appears, not sure.
	- There are two scaffolding routes up to the roof:
		- Run straight through the shopping mall to the big plaza with the fountain; hang right and climb the scaffolding and hop on to the roof.
		- Run through the shopping mall and exit right, then down the alley past the 'old house entrance' to the far side of the plaza. Go left at the end and climb the scaffold to the teleporter (part of the scaffold is clambering on brown boxes in a kinda buggy way).
	- Once on the roof, you can probably roto-glide over to the other roof, or you can cross the bridge; then you can hop down to the arena.

	- Just find an entrance to the shopping center and run in. I got this one early - I don't remember if some of the entrances are blocked or whatever.
